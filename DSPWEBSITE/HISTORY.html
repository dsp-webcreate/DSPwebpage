History of Dominican School of Pilar

In 1953, the Tabuena Family opened Pilar Institute as a branch of Bicol College, Daraga, Albay. But for some reasons, in 1968, the Tabuenas decided to sell the school. Rev. Fr. Vicente Romano, the Parish Priest of Pilar, consulted the bishop of Sorsogon. His proposal was approved, and he was tasked with negotiating with the owner and religious congregations.

Mother Teresita Hacbang, O.P., then Prioress General of the Dominican Sisters of St. Catherine of Siena, was offered the schoolâ€™s administration. After evaluation and several conferences, the school was purchased, and the Dominican Sisters began operating it â€” becoming the first and only Catholic school in Pilar.

The pioneering sisters were Sr. Norberta Orbita, OP (Superior), Sr. Violeta Estrada, OP (Principal), and Sr. Priscilla Faraon, OP (Treasurer). Despite limited facilities and funding, they worked diligently to grow the school.

In 1980, a new two-story building was built, housing offices, faculty rooms, a library, laboratory, and quarters. Due to financial constraints, it wasn't fully finished until 1988.

In 1987, Typhoon Sisang destroyed both buildings. Though the congregation considered closing the school, generous benefactors helped rebuild. By June 1988, two new buildings with seven classrooms were finished, and the third floor of an earlier building was added.

Today, the school continues to grow under the OP-Siena Schools Systems Development Program (OP-SSDP), promoting a Christian learning community inspired by Dominican values and the ideals of Mother Francisca del Espiritu Santo de Fuentes, the foundress.
